What is Threshold %?

What are the odds you've seen enough sites to play that card.

Every spell in Sorcery requires a certain number of element pips (threshold) to cast. Your sites provide those pips. Each turn you reveal sites from your atlas — the calculator tells you the probability that you've revealed enough of the right element by the turn you want to cast that spell.

It uses the multivariate hypergeometric distribution — the same math as drawing colored balls from an urn without replacement, but smarter: it knows that a 2-pip site gives you 2 pips in a single draw.

The Formula

The concept

Your atlas has three kinds of cards for any given element: cards with 0 pips, cards with 1 pip, and cards with 2 pips. You draw n cards. The calculator counts every possible combination of draws and checks which ones give you enough total pips.

P(total pips ≥ k)  =  1 − ∑ j1+2·j2 < k
C(K1, j1) × C(K2, j2) × C(K0, n−j1−j2)  /  C(N, n)

In plain words

Sum up every way to draw cards that don't give you enough pips, then subtract from 1.

For each bad draw: ways to pick j1 one-pip cards × ways to pick j2 two-pip cards × ways to fill the rest with zero-pip cards, divided by all possible draws.

Variables

Symbol Name Meaning Example
N Atlas size Total number of cards in your atlas 15 cards
K1 1-pip cards Cards with exactly 1 pip of the element (e.g. Aqueduct has 1 fire) 3 cards
K2 2-pip cards Cards with exactly 2 pips of the element (e.g. Active Volcano has 2 fire) 3 cards
K0 0-pip cards Cards with no pips of this element (N − K1 − K2) 9 cards
n Sites seen How many cards you've revealed by this turn (opening hand + draws) 5 by turn 3
k Pips needed The threshold requirement on the spell 2 for a (FF) spell
C(a, b) Combinations "a choose b" — ways to pick b items from a items C(6,2) = 15

Why This Matters

A simpler formula would count each pip as a separate "ball" in the urn. But that's wrong — a 2-pip site like Active Volcano gives you both pips in one draw. The old approach overestimates your probability.

Example: 15-card atlas, 3× Active Volcano (2F), 3× Aqueduct (1F), 5 sites seen, need 1 fire pip.
Naive (counting pips as balls, K=9): 99.8%
Accurate (counting cards, K1=3, K2=3): 95.8%
The naive approach inflates K from 6 cards to 9 "pips", making it seem like you have more sources than you do.

What Modifies the Calculation

The core formula uses the variables above. Several game mechanics adjust them before the probability is computed:

Modifier What it changes How
Archetype n Aggro, Midrange, or Pathfinder — each sees a different number of sites per turn
Elementalist k Reduces every element's required pips by 1
Special sites K1 Annual Fair, Valley of Delight, Floodplain — add full or fractional 1-pip sources
Spellbook sources K1 Cores, Families, Shrine — combined probability with atlas (spellbook always provides 1 pip each)
Site-draw cards n Land Surveyor, Landmass, etc. — adds extra sites seen based on the probability of having drawn the card from your spellbook by turn 3 (roughly 5 spells seen)
Multi-element spells result Each element's probability is computed independently, then multiplied together
Multi-element sites K1 A site like Aqueduct (1F 1W) counts as a 1-pip card for fire AND a 1-pip card for water — each element is calculated independently

About This Project

This calculator was built to answer a simple question every Sorcery player asks when building a deck: "Do I have enough sources to reliably cast this spell?"
